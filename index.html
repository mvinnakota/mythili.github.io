About Me

About Me



I am a doctoral student at the Harris School of Public Policy at the University of Chicago. I am interested in using applied microeconomics and empirical methods to better understand how poverty and inequality are affected by climate change and natural disasters.


My other research interests include environmental justice and housing markets. I am also interested in thinking about normative economics and how equity could be better addressed by economics research.


I am a New Jersey native who currently calls Chicago my (second) home. When I'm not working on my doctoral degree, you can find me trying out new recipes or reading Margaret Atwood novels.
