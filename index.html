About Me

About Me



I am a doctoral student at the Harris School of Public Policy at the University of Chicago. I am interested in using applied microeconomics and empirical methods to better understand how poverty and inequality are affected by climate change and natural disasters.


Specifically, I am working on a few empirical projects that look at how renters are impacted by climate-related housing market shifts, and how homeowners internalize home maintenance costs from extreme weather events.


I am a native New Jerseyan who currently calls Chicago my (second) home. When I'm not working on my doctoral degree, you can find me learning new dance styles, trying out new recipes, or reading Margaret Atwood novels.
